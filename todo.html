1. Cancún Underwater Museum of Art(MUSA)

At this museum it is a little bit different than your typical visit anywhere else. Here you will encounter a more interactive,close up and hands on experience like no where else in the world that will leave you astonished in amazement. There are over 45 modules and sculptures to witness and several ways to do so, including: Snorkeling, Scuba Diving, our Glass Bottom Boat tour or even our Jungle tour.

There are two general locations where the statues and sculptures are located one being in Salon, Manchones which is located off the coast of Isla Mujeres sitting roughly 8 meters deep, therefore being reccomended for the scuba Diving portion only. The other location is Punta Nizuc, which is located at the southern point of Cancún's hotel zone and this location is estimated at around 4 meters deep so is accessible by our snorkeling tour.

Cost: Anywhere from $40 minimum to $140 maximum
Age Group: Ages 6 and older

2. Xcaret Amusement Park

Xcaret is a water, theme, amusement, eco-archaeological park and more all wrapped up into one place. It is located in the Mayan jungle around the underground rivers, cenotes and faced towards the Caribbean Sea. You can do different activities and interact in impressive scenarios allowing you to enjoy the park to the fullest. Xcaret also celebrates Mexico's present and rich past, a country known for its traditions, culture and folklore.

Here you can have fun in the water activities that will take you to territories of Chaac, the Mayan God of water. You can enjoy the underground rivers, the lagoon, beach area and the natural pools of sea water. If you brought children there is also a portion of the park desiged especially for the little ones, it is called Children's World, here they can swim in the shallow cenotes and wading pools constructed just for them.

Also you can come and visit our Coral Reef Aquarium and the sea turtle center. You can learn more about the regional flora and fauna at the Butterfly Garden, the aviary the Rainforest Trail and the living Orchid and Bromeliad Museum. 3. Isla Contoy

This beautiful uninhabited island is located approximately 30 Kilometers north of Isla Mujeres. The island itself is only 8.5 Km in length. It is also considered to be the most important nesting place of sea birds in all of the Mexican Caribbean.

The island holds more than a hundred and fifty migrating and resident bird species. The most numerous being the frigate, brown pelican and double-crested cormorant. It is also an essential nesting spot for Sea Turtles. Not only are the species of birds beautiful but the beach is sandy white and it is secluded. Since the island is not very large there is a max capacity of 200 visitors allowed daily. The beach is accessible by a tour that sails directly out of Cancún roughly taking one to two hours to get to depending on the weather and boat type. If your are looking for more information or are interested in booking a tour you should visit our web page down below.

4.Ventura Park

The perfect amusement park fit for the whole family! Here there is several things to encounter. A great place to start is at "Wet 'n Wild", where you can indulge in several water activities most of which include water slides of many different sizes and several pools. The perfect place to cool off and have a relaxing swim. If you want a taste of wildlife there is also "Dolphinaris", which is Cancún's #1 option for dolphin swims.

If you are looking for some serious adventure you should visit "Aaa". In this section of the park you can enjoy ziplines and bungee swings. Any one with small children will love "Fun World", where the activities are all child friendly that adults and youth can enjoy. There is a Grand Prix section that allows you to drive on a go-kart track that is completely seaside and there is an arcade which offers state-of-the-art activities like virtual reality and several game options.
